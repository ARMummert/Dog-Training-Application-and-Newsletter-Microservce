Touch Command

The touch command is one of the first commands you should teach your dog. Dog's use their noses to explore the world around them. Sniffing helps a dog understand their surroundings and the people they encounter.

You will need a clicker or some form of reward to perform this command. The reward can be a toy only provided to your dog during your training sessions or a highly rewarding food treat.

Follow the guide to get started:

  1. With your dog in front of you, hold your hand, palm up, out towards your dog
  2. Hold a reward in the other hand.
  3. Give the command: Touch
  4. When your dog sniffs your hand, give the reward (or click), and say "Good Touch"
  5. Repeat ten times a training session until your dog has mastered the touch command.

Sit

The sit command uses the command and reward system. Teaching your dog to sit can be very rewarding, not only for you but for your dog as well.

You will need a clicker or some form of reward to perform this command. We recommend using a leash during your first training sessions so you can work with your dog with them running amok.

Follow the guide to get started:

  1. With your dog in front of you, hold the reward in your hand and place it in front of your dogs nose.
  2. Slowly lift your hand with the reward over your dogs head towards their tail.
  3. Give the command: Sit
  4. When your dog sits give them the reward and say "Good Sit"
  5. Repeat ten times a training session until your dog has mastered the sit command.

Down

There are coulpe of options for starting to teach your dog down. You can start with the touch command or by by placing your hand, palm down, in front of your dog and proceed with the down command.

It's important to remember that dogs lay down in different ways. The goal of the down command is to get your dog to roll their hips to the side rather than their legs under their hips. Some dogs will lay down but with their legs underneath their hips.

Follow the guide to get started:

  1. Start with the touch command to get your dog positioned in front of you and ready to train.
  2. Hold your hand out, palms down, and slowly move your hand down towards the ground while giving the down command.
  3. Give the command: Down (while moving your hand towards the ground).
  4. When your dog lays down say "Down"
  5. Repeat ten times a training session until your dog has mastered the down command.

Stay

The stay command might seem scary but it's actually pretty simple to teach. This command is an important one. Not only can it keep your dog safe but it could prevent your dog from jumping on people, impulsively running off, or just keeping them in one location in your home for a few moments.

Follow the guide to get started:

  1. Stand in front of your dog and give the sit command.
  2. Hold your hand out in front of you, like the stop signal.
  3. Give the command: Down
  4. Wait just a moment before rewarding, and say "Good Stay"
  5. When you first start training stay, start with just a few seconds then increase the time each training session that you ask them to stay.
  6. Repeat ten times a training session until your dog has mastered the stay command.
